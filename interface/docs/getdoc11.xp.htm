Sandro Wenzel wrote in message <3789A647.BCCBF44A@fhtw-berlin.de>...
>I have 2 questions about IRQs in a vxd and Win95:
>
>1) I understand the DDK-Help, that the masking/unmasking of the PIC will
>be automatically done. But if the assigned IRQ not used by another
>device too (IRQ is shareable), it doesn't work. If the IRQ is used by
>another device too, the PIC is already masked and it works. Is it a good
>way to mask the PIC manually everytime using VPICD_Physically_Mask? The
>VPICD_Physically_Unmask function is critical, because if another device
>using my IRQ I disable this device if I calling this function before I
>close my driver...
>
>2) Should I use VPICD_Phys_EOI only if my device was the source of the
>(shared) IRQ? Or should I use it in every case at the end of my
>interrupt routine?
  
Microsoft has confused these issues a lot by deciding long ago that VxD programmers should not be trusted to understand that the PIC is being driven in what Intel's literature calls Special Mask Mode.
 
When your interrupt routine is called, the current request has been ended (i.e., the PIC has been sent an EOI) but the IRQ level is masked. Interrupts may occur at IRQ levels above and below yours. When you can stand to be reentered, you unmask your IRQ level at the PIC. However, you do this by calling the VPICD service that is named VPICD_Phys_EOI.
 
Treat VPICD_Physically_Mask as permanent masking - well, permanent until the next call to VPICD_Physically_Unmask.
 
The interaction between these services is fairly simple. Imagine that there is a logical Interrupt Service Register (ISR) and a logical Interrupt Mask Register (IMR). Occurrence of your interrupt sets the logical ISR. Calling VPICD_Phys_EOI clears the logical ISR. Calling VPICD_Physically_Mask sets the logical IMR. Calling VPICD_Physically_Unmask clears the logical IMR. At the PIC itself, the true ISR is always clear whenever your code can execute. When you call any of the three services, the true IMR is set to the OR of the logical ISR and logical IMR.
 
From this, you can make deductions:
 
Suppose you are in an interrupt routine. If you call VPICD_Physically_Mask, then a later VPICD_Phys_EOI does not unmask your IRQ level at the PIC. Interrupts cannot occur at the IRQ level. They are masked.
 
Suppose you are in an interrupt routine and you call
VPICD_Physically_Unmask, then the IRQ level is not actually unmasked at the PIC until you call VPICD_Phys_EOI. Even though you seem to have unmasked the IRQ level, interrupts cannot recur at your level until you call
VPICD_Phys_EOI.
 
And so it goes.
 
One point to watch is that masking your IRQ level really does mean masking your IRQ level. You mask against recurrence of interrupts at that same IRQ level even from devices that you share the IRQ level with.
